my work

I'm a member of the ATLAS experiment at CERN (European Center for Nuclear Research). I apply my programming skills and analytical techniques to improve the efficiency of current algorithms for the classification of elementary particles. At Yale, I worked with Prof. Tobias Golling on the design and application of Deep Learning algorithms to develop taggers for the identification of top quarks and gauge bosons in boosted topologies. I am currently working on a qualification task to optimize b-tagging techniques, by including track-jets for Run II at the LHC (Large Hadron Collider). My previous research experiences included theoretical, observational and computational work in galactic dynamics, Astrophysics, and space weather.
